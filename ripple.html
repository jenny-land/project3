01 Overview
Context
A 48-hour hackathon design sprint at Diablo Valley College. Ripple is an iOS mobile app designed to help people with chronic illness, disability, and fluctuating energy conditions navigate social planning with honesty and ease — without requiring medical explanation every time.
My Role
Led all research and synthesis, defined the core interaction model and aura visualization concept, built the complete design system ("Soft Morning"), designed multi-state UI across all screens, and created the competition presentation deck.
Methods
Semi-structured interviews · Affinity mapping · Thematic analysis · Competitive analysis · Heuristic evaluation · Literature review (allostasis research) · Persona development · Accessibility audit
Outcome
Complete interactive Figma prototype with a full research foundation, multi-state UI, and competition-ready presentation deck — delivered within 48 hours. The aura system was cited by judges as a novel approach to ambient social communication.
02 The Problem

Every major social coordination tool was built on a silent assumption: that users have consistent, reliable energy. For the estimated 1 in 5 people managing chronic conditions — POTS, ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, ADHD — that assumption creates a painful social dynamic that existing tools do nothing to address.

People with fluctuating energy face the same impossible choice in every social interaction: overshare medical details to justify a cancellation, mask their actual state to appear capable, or quietly withdraw from social life altogether. None of these options support authentic connection — and none of the tools on the market were designed with this reality in mind.

Core Problem Statement

How might we help people with fluctuating energy communicate their social availability authentically — without requiring them to explain their condition every time?

03 Research & Discovery

Given the 48-hour constraint, the research phase prioritized depth over breadth. I conducted 7 semi-structured interviews with people living with chronic illness, POTS, ME/CFS, or other energy-variable conditions — recruited through a personal health advocacy network I'm part of.

Findings were synthesized in a live FigJam affinity mapping session, organized across four clusters: Energy Unpredictability, Social Pressure, Communication Barriers, and Coping Strategies. The research also drew on allostasis literature to build a conceptual grounding for the aura metaphor — framing energy as dynamic and contextual rather than a depleting battery.

The most revealing finding wasn't what people lacked — it was what they had already built. Participants had invented sophisticated, informal systems for communicating energy: color-coded emoji with close friends, pre-negotiated check-in phrases, private shorthand. Ripple's opportunity was to give those grassroots behaviors a home.

I also conducted a competitive analysis of 5 existing apps — social planning tools, health trackers, and accessibility-focused products — and a heuristic evaluation of existing tools. None addressed the core problem of ambient, low-effort energy communication.

Maya · Primary Persona · POTS
"I either have to lie and say I'm fine, or overshare my entire medical history just to cancel lunch."
The binary choice between oversharing and masking reveals a missing middle — a way to communicate truthfully without clinical language or detailed justification.
Jordan · Secondary Persona · ME/CFS
"Every RSVP feels like a contract I might have to break, and I hate myself when I do."
The emotional weight of cancellation — guilt, shame, unreliability — is a design problem. The right system reframes uncertainty as honest communication, not failure.
Key Research Finding

Users weren't looking for a better calendar. They had already invented the system they needed — in emoji, code words, and trusted shorthand. Ripple's job was to formalize what already existed, not design from scratch.

04 Process
1
Research Sprint
7 interviews in 4 hours. Live affinity mapping to identify behavioral clusters before any sketching. Literature review of allostasis research ran in parallel to ground the energy metaphor scientifically.
2
Concept & Principles
Established 4 design principles before wireframing: low cognitive load, emotionally honest, dignified communication, inclusive by default. Principles acted as decision filters throughout the sprint — not decoration.
3
Design System First
Built the "Soft Morning" design system before individual screens — palette, typography, spacing, motion, and illustration principles. Visual language aligned with the emotional tone before a single screen was designed.
4
Prototype & Refine
Built a multi-state Figma prototype covering 6 core screens in full and low-energy variants. Reduced the energy scale from 5 states to 3 based on cognitive load feedback. Presentation deck designed in the same system.

The Challenge
The aura visualization is visually expressive and emotionally resonant — but an animated ambient glow is completely inaccessible for screen reader users. Ripple's target population includes people who rely on VoiceOver and other assistive technology at low-energy moments, specifically when the app is most needed.
The Decision
The aura was designed as a visual layer over a text-based energy state system. The primary data is always text: "full energy," "taking it easy," "offline today." The aura is the enhancement — not the source of truth. Screen readers read the text state. The visual system earns its place on top of a working accessible foundation.
Accessibility-first meant the visual system had to earn its place on top of a working text foundation — not replace it.
05 Final Design

The final prototype centers on the home screen aura — a soft animated glow that reflects the user's current energy state. The aura isn't decorative; it's the primary communication surface. When a user checks in, the aura shifts in intensity, color, and rhythm to reflect their state across all their connections simultaneously, with no message required.

One of the most intentional screens in the prototype is the low-energy home state. When a user checks in at low capacity, the interface softens: the aura dims, quick-action tiles reduce to a single "Rest Mode" option, and notification badges disappear. The screen communicates that rest is acceptable — no prompts to improve, no red alerts, no productivity pressure.

06 Outcomes
Judges cited the aura system as the most novel interaction model in the hackathon — the only entry addressing ambient social communication for people with fluctuating energy conditions.
Complete prototype delivered in 48 hours — full research foundation, multi-state UI, "Soft Morning" design system, and competition-ready presentation deck.
Accessibility-first architecture validated — text-based energy states confirmed functional with screen reader before any visual aura layer was applied. WCAG 2.1 AA met throughout; key elements reach AAA.
Ripple is continuing as a Spring 2025 portfolio piece with extended research, higher-fidelity prototyping, and a formal usability testing phase currently in planning.
What this taught me

Designing for a community you're personally part of is both an advantage and a risk. I had to work harder to separate my own experience from what participants actually said — and that discipline made the research more honest and the design more grounded.

What Worked
Establishing design principles before wireframing. "Inclusive by default" and "emotionally honest" prevented major rework — every screen decision had a filter to run it through. The aura metaphor also came directly from the research, not from aesthetics. That grounding made it defensible.
What I'd Change
With more time I'd test the energy scale more rigorously — 3 states vs. 5 vs. a continuous slider. The decision to use 3 was informed by cognitive load feedback but not formally validated. I'd also want to test the "soft commitment" language in the Plan Together screen with users outside the chronic illness community.
Next Steps
Extend research to 15+ participants. Prototype the notification system — how does Ripple communicate a friend's energy change without creating anxiety? Run a formal accessibility audit with screen reader users. Build a high-fidelity version for the full portfolio case study.
